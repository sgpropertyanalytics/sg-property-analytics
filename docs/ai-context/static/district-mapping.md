# District and Region Mapping

## Market Segments (Regions)

Singapore's private residential market is divided into three segments based on URA classification:

### CCR (Core Central Region)
Premium/prime districts. Highest land values, luxury developments, proximity to Orchard Road and CBD.

**Districts**: D01, D02, D06, D07, D09, D10, D11

**Characteristics**:
- Highest PSF in Singapore
- Significant foreign buyer presence
- Luxury and ultra-luxury developments
- Strong rental demand from expats

### RCR (Rest of Central Region)
City fringe districts. Good connectivity, mature estates, mix of old and new developments.

**Districts**: D03, D04, D05, D08, D12, D13, D14, D15, D20

**Characteristics**:
- Mid-tier pricing
- Popular with upgraders
- Mix of freehold and leasehold
- Strong local buyer base

### OCR (Outside Central Region)
Suburban districts. Mass market developments, HDB upgrader catchment.

**Districts**: D16, D17, D18, D19, D21, D22, D23, D24, D25, D26, D27, D28

**Characteristics**:
- Most affordable private housing
- Predominantly 99-year leasehold
- Family-oriented developments
- Price-sensitive buyers

## Interpretation Notes

When comparing prices across regions:
- CCR vs OCR spread indicates market health and risk appetite
- Narrowing spread may signal OCR overheating or CCR weakness
- Regional performance often diverges based on buyer demographics and policy impacts

When a specific district is mentioned, always contextualize within its region for proper benchmarking.
